About Viv

Viv is a creative artificial intelligence that attempts to capture and model creative inspiration, generating 3D-printable vases that are inspired by a source image. The system analyzes an image and extracts a color palette consisting of the most common and most noticeable colors in the image, then uses these colors to build up a set of aesthetic characteristics (hardness, activity, warmth, and weight) for the image based on research in color psychology. Viv creatively make vases based on inspiration from the color and composition of an image, whose resulting shape should evoke similar feelings as that of the image.

The core innovation from this research is an artificially intelligent agent which creates 3D vases using a novel domain-knowledge transfer method. By reproducing inherent human psychological responses that are shared by both color and form, the VIV A.I. transforms 2-Dimensional image color into the form of a 3-Dimensional vase. Vases created by VIV may be ranked by the user for accuracy creating data to further refine the A.I. Additionally demographical data is collected to gain a richer understanding of the human psychology that connects color and form.

Our vision for this research is to enable a multitude of artificially intelligent systems which can create objects and assets based on artistic inspiration from another seemingly unrelated domain. Converting expertise from one domain to another is a common problem for both humans and computers and we hope this research can gain insight into more efficient transfer methods.

The ongoing Viv project is a collaboration between students and faculty in the College of Arts, Media and Design and the College of Computer and Information Science. While previous research has been able to create content generation systems and cross-domain knowledge sharing mechanisms, no previous research to our knowledge has combined these ideas to allow a computer to be creative during content generation. The ongoing Viv project, established in early 2014, is a collaboration between students and faculty in the College of Arts, Media and Design and the College of Computer and Information Science.
